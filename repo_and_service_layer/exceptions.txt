What is a Custom Exception?

Instead of raising a generic exception: raise Exception("User not found")

or a framework-specific exception: raise HTTPException(...)

we create exceptions that represent our business domain.
Examples:

- UserNotFoundError
- EmailAlreadyExistsError
- InvalidCredentialsError
- InsufficientBalanceError
- OrderAlreadyShippedError
- Notice something.
- These names describe the problem, not the HTTP response.

Folder Structure
app/
│
├── exceptions/
│   ├── __init__.py
│   └── user.py
│
├── handlers/
│   └── exception_handlers.py
│
├── services/
├── repositories/
├── routers/
└── main.py

Why separate them?

exceptions/ defines what can go wrong.
handlers/ defines how HTTP clients should see those errors.
